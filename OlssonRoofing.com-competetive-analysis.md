## **Strategic Recommendations for Olsson Roofing**

To maintain its dominant market position and adapt to the evolving digital landscape, Olsson Roofing should consider the following strategic improvements.

### **1\. Develop a "Commercial Knowledge Center"**

Olsson should transition its content strategy from "Internal News" to "External Education." By leveraging its 100+ years of data, it can create authoritative guides on the lifecycle costs of various roofing membranes.

The Total Cost of Ownership ($TCO$) of a roofing system can be mathematically expressed as:

$$TCO \= C\_{initial} \+ \\sum\_{t=1}^{n} \\frac{M\_t \+ R\_t}{(1+r)^t} \- \\frac{S}{(1+r)^n}$$  
Where:

* $C\_{initial}$ is the initial installation cost.  
* $M\_t$ and $R\_t$ are maintenance and repair costs in year $t$.  
* $r$ is the discount rate.  
* $S$ is the salvage value or energy savings (e.g., from a "green" roof).  
* $n$ is the expected life of the roof in years.

By providing calculators or data tables that help facility managers perform this analysis, Olsson can position itself as a financial partner, not just a service provider.

### **2\. Implement the "Lakeland Process" for Industrial Clients**

Olsson should define a "Olsson Industrial Lifecycle" on its website, mapping out the journey from Initial Thermal Drone Scan to preventative maintenance and eventual replacement. This visual map would help normalize the engagement for new procurement officers who may be intimidated by the scale of the company.

### **3\. Aggressive Localized Landing Pages**

Following the strategy used by Lakeland, Olsson should create dedicated landing pages for major industrial hubs like the Aurora West Commercial Park and the Cherry Valley industrial corridor. These pages should feature project galleries specific to those cities to boost local search relevance.

### **4\. Leverage the "In-House" Advantage**

Olsson should more aggressively market their "Employee-Only" workforce and their in-house metal fabrication hub in Romeoville. In an era of rampant subcontracting (which Apex markets against), Olsson’s ability to control every variable of the project is a massive, under-marketed asset.

### **5\. Modernize Review Acquisition**

The company should implement an automated review acquisition system that prompts clients (especially those who have left the high-quality testimonials currently on the site) to post on Google Maps and Angi. This will build the "Search Engine Trust" necessary to compete with the high-volume review counts of Apex and Lakeland.

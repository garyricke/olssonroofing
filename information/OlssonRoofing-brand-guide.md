## **Olsson Roofing Brand Guide (Draft v1)**

### **1\) Brand snapshot**

**Who you are:** One of the oldest and largest commercial roofing companies in Illinois, founded in **1914**, with **two locations**, **200+ annual projects** across the Midwest, and a **24/7 service team** responding to **4,000+ requests/year**.  
**What you do:** Commercial/industrial roofing (new construction, replacement, recover/overlay, green roofing), plus architectural sheet metal through **Metal Edge** (design/fabricate/install; significant in-house production).  
**Why you win:** Scale \+ logistics \+ institutional-grade safety \+ verified manufacturer excellence (“Hall of Fame”/elite-tier recognition across major manufacturers).

---

### **2\) Positioning**

**Positioning statement (internal):**  
*Olsson Roofing is the Midwest’s institutional-scale commercial roofing and building-envelope partner—trusted for mission-critical uptime, meticulous planning, and a safety culture that protects people, schedules, and facilities.*

**Primary audiences**

* Facility directors & property managers (industrial, institutional, healthcare, pharma, logistics, education)  
* General contractors & construction managers (schedule \+ coordination heavy projects)  
* Data center / “mission critical” stakeholders (hyperscale & live environments)

**Brand promise**

* “Done right the first time” with safe, timely, reliable information through closeout.  
* Fast dispatch \+ experienced repair outcomes \+ safer execution (“faster / better / safer”).  
  ---

  ### **3\) Brand pillars (messaging foundations)**

1. **Safety is a product** (and it protects uptime)  
   * Emphasis on safety standards \+ “safe return home” mindset.  
2. **Institutional scale & responsiveness**  
   * Largest staff for commercial repair → fast dispatch; high annual service volume.  
3. **Single point of accountability**  
   * Simple process, clear coordination, dependable project communication.  
4. **Total envelope capability (roof \+ metal)**  
   * Metal Edge: design/manufacture/install \+ significant custom production in-house.  
5. **Mission critical & hyperscale readiness**  
   * Dedicated technicians; live/active data center replacements without interruption.

   ---

   ### **4\) Voice & tone**

**Brand voice:** Confident, disciplined, plain-spoken, professional. “Field-tested,” not flowery.  
**Tone range:**

* **Sales/website:** authoritative \+ reassuring (risk reduction, uptime protection)  
* **Service/emergency:** calm \+ decisive \+ fast  
* **Careers:** proud, team-first, long-tenure, “build careers not just jobs”

**Words/phrases to lean on (existing equity)**

* “We get to you faster. We fix it better. We work safer.”  
* “Nobody cares about roofing like we do.”  
* “Olsson on Top.” (heritage tagline)  
* “Integrity, continuous improvement, customer service, professionalism.”

  ## **Visual Identity**

  ### **6\) Logo usage (rules of thumb)**

**Primary mark:** Use the full Olsson Roofing lockup whenever possible (most recognizable).  
**Clear space:** Keep at least the height of the “O” (or a similar internal unit) around all sides.  
**Minimum size:**

* Digital: don’t use below \~140px wide for the full lockup; create a simplified mark for small favicon/app use.  
* Print: don’t use below \~1.25" wide for the full lockup.  
  **Backgrounds:**  
* Preferred: white or very light neutral.  
* Dark backgrounds: use white logo where possible; if using the red mark, ensure strong contrast.

### **7\) Color palette (pulled directly from the attached logo SVG)**

Your SVG contains these core brand colors: **Olsson Red**, **Deep Brick**, and two dark neutrals. (Extracted from `/mnt/data/olsson-roofing-logo.svg`.)

#### **Core palette**

* **Olsson Red (Primary): \#E9322E** — hero accents, key calls-to-action, highlights  
  * CMYK (approx): 0 / 79 / 80 / 9  
* **Deep Brick (Secondary): \#802B26** — premium/heritage accent, headers on light backgrounds  
  * CMYK (approx): 0 / 66 / 70 / 50  
* **Charcoal (Primary Neutral): \#161616** — headings, body text, iconography  
  * CMYK (approx): 0 / 0 / 0 / 91  
* **Slate (Secondary Neutral): \#333333** — secondary text, dividers, UI neutrals  
  * CMYK (approx): 0 / 0 / 0 / 80

  #### **Extended tints (recommended for backgrounds & UI)**

Derived from Olsson Red to keep the brand feel consistent:

* **Red-Soft:** \#F0706D (subtle cards, hover states)  
* **Red-Blush:** \#F49896 (background banding)  
* **Red-Mist:** \#F8C2C0 (very light sections)  
* **Red-Fog:** \#FCE0E0 (barely-there tint)

  #### **Accessibility note (practical)**

* **\#E9322E on white** is borderline for normal-sized body text. Use **\#C92E2A** (a slightly darker “Link Red”) for small text links to improve readability, while keeping **\#E9322E** for buttons/badges and large display usage.  
  ---

  ### **8\) Typography (web \+ brand systems)**

To match “industrial-grade / institutional / modern,” keep typography clean and technical:

* **Headlines:** *Montserrat* or *Oswald* (confident, condensed options work well for construction brands)  
* **Body:** *Inter* or *Source Sans 3* (high legibility, modern)  
* **Numbers/specs:** Use body font with tabular numerals if available (helps bids, measurements, dates)  
  ---

  ### **9\) Photography & graphic style**

**Photo direction:** real crews, real scale, real sites; emphasize safety culture, clean job sites, and “mission critical” environments (data centers, hospitals, pharma) to reinforce trust. (This aligns with your current site themes: safety leadership, major projects, and long-tenure people stories.)  
**Graphic motifs:** simple grid layouts, strong contrast, minimal ornamentation; use red as an accent—not a flood.

---

### **10\) Quick “brand guardrails” (so everything stays consistent)**

**Do**

* Lead with credibility: longevity \+ scale \+ safety \+ responsiveness.  
* Keep copy concrete: who, what, where, how fast, how safe.  
* Use red strategically (CTAs, badges, key stats).

**Don’t**

* Overpromise with vague superlatives (“best ever”) without proof points.  
* Use multiple bright accent colors that compete with Olsson Red.  
* Use playful/quirky tone in mission critical or safety contexts.

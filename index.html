Safety Culture · EMR · Safetyfest · OSHA

Safety at Olsson Roofing

At Olsson Roofing, safety isn't a program — it's a deeply embedded organizational value that predates modern OSHA requirements by decades. Our safety record is one of the most powerful competitive advantages we hold, and it directly benefits every client, every project, and every crew member.

Experience Modification Rate (EMR)

The EMR is the construction industry's primary actuarial safety measure. A rating of 1.0 is the industry average. A rating below 1.0 means a contractor has fewer and less severe workers' compensation claims than the norm — and pays significantly lower insurance premiums as a result.

Olsson Roofing consistently maintains an EMR well below 1.0. For our clients, this translates directly to:

  • Lower overall project risk and insurance-related costs
  • Pre-qualification eligibility for Fortune 500 and institutional contracts that require contractors with a sub-1.0 EMR
  • Confidence that the team on your roof is trained, disciplined, and supervised to the highest standard
  • Fewer schedule disruptions caused by on-site incidents

Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) & DART

Olsson's TRIR — the number of recordable incidents per 100 full-time workers — consistently places us in the "Top Performer" category (below 1.0). Our DART rate (Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred) reflects the same discipline. Clients such as Abbott Laboratories and AbbVie monitor both metrics as a non-negotiable prerequisite for any roofing contractor working on their campuses.

Fleet Safety: Zero Crashes on 1.6 Million Miles

Olsson operates a private fleet of 51+ power units that covers more than 1.6 million miles annually across the Midwest. According to FMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration) data, Olsson Roofing has recorded zero crashes — fatal, injury, or tow-away — in the 24 months preceding 2026. For a fleet of that scale and mileage, this is a statistically remarkable achievement and a direct reflection of our driver training programs and vehicle maintenance standards.

CRCA Gold Medal Safety Award

The Chicago Roofing Contractors Association (CRCA) Gold Medal Safety Award is the highest safety honor in the Illinois commercial roofing industry. Olsson Roofing has received this award three times — in 2007, 2018, and 2025 — a distinction held by no other contractor in our market. The award recognizes sustained excellence in jobsite safety, training program quality, and EMR performance over multiple consecutive years.

OSHA National Stand Down to Prevent Falls

Each year, Olsson Roofing participates in OSHA's National Stand Down to Prevent Falls in Construction — the industry's most important annual safety event. During Stand Down Week, all field operations pause for dedicated safety training sessions focused on fall prevention, PPE use, and hazard recognition. Olsson has partnered with OSHA's Naperville Area Office and industry safety experts to deliver training that goes well beyond the regulatory minimum.

  • Fall arrest system inspection and proper harness fitting
  • Leading-edge work protocols
  • Ladder safety and roof access procedures
  • Toolbox talk facilitation for foremen and crew leads

Safetyfest

Safetyfest is a multi-day regional safety education event bringing together contractors, safety professionals, and OSHA representatives. Olsson Roofing has been a consistent participant and presenter, with company leaders including President Mike Reynolds taking the stage to share Olsson's safety philosophy with the broader industry. Our crew members attend sessions on new regulations, emerging best practices, and hands-on skill refreshers.

The Safetyfest pledge board — signed by every attending crew member — represents Olsson's collective personal commitment: every person goes home safe, every single day.

The 5 P's Philosophy

Proper Planning Prevents Poor Performance. This motto, championed by Olsson's field leaders, captures the core of our safety approach: safety incidents are almost always the result of inadequate planning, not bad luck. By building safety review into every pre-job meeting, daily toolbox talk, and project closeout, Olsson ensures that hazards are identified and mitigated before work begins — not after an incident occurs.

Pharmaceutical & Mission-Critical Environments

Working in active pharmaceutical facilities (Abbott, AbbVie) and live data centers demands a safety standard that goes beyond OSHA baseline requirements. Olsson's protocols for these environments include:

  • No-odor, cold-applied installation methods to protect indoor air quality
  • Dust and debris containment systems to prevent cleanroom contamination
  • Strict badge-in / badge-out access control integration with client security
  • TRIR and DART documentation provided to facility managers on request
  • Pre-task planning (PTP) forms completed and submitted before any scope change
